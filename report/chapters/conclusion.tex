\section{Design Data}
As a conclusion we report here the relevant data of the whole design:
It takes in total 6308 LUTs, 9946 Flip Flops, 54 BRAMs and 60 DSPs.
It has a 0.369 ns of Worst Negative Slack and an estimated total on-chip power
of 0.511 W.
The motor has an estimated power utilization of 16.08 W while in use.
\section{Future work}
As a next step one could modify the design to lower the FFT resolution to use less area but
most importantly less time to fill the buffer, to give the user the experience of having a continuous
signal analysis rather than a discrete one.
One could also try to make the FFT streaming rather than waiting for the buffer, or Radix burst to use less area.
It is also possible to lower the sampling frequency with the benefits and drawbacks described in Chapter~\ref{chapter:sig}.

Once the structure is built, building new feature on it is much easier. You could add more motors to tune every
string together (although it comes with some changes to the signal processing part), or add a more interactive UI; limit's the sky.

\paragraph{}
In conclusion one can experiment tuning this parameters to achieve the goal they want, or build new features on it
but this design stays moreover the same.
This report can serve as a guideline to whomever wants to improve it.
