The integration of \textbf{digital signal processing} (DSP) and \textbf{automated
systems} can offer solutions to possible real-world challenges.
One example of said use cases involves the world of music, where the tuning
of stringed instruments can be a frequent and tedious task for musicians.
While digital tuners can provide visual feedback, they still require manual adjustment
by the performer.
This project introduces an autonomous, hardware-driven solution: an automatic guitar
tuning system implemented on a \textbf{Xilinx Zedboard Development Kit} using \textbf{VHDL}.

The audio received directly from the guitar is processed thanks to a \textbf{Fast Fourier Transform} (FFT) algorithm,
which identifies the fundamental frequency of the string being played.
Such frequency is then compared to the desired target frequency, and allows the system to
determine the necessary adjustments to bring the string into tune.

\paragraph{}
By bypassing general-purpose microprocessor and implementing the core logic directly in the \textbf{Programmable Logic} (PL) of the Zedboard,
the design shows a possible full ASIC integration for the time audio analysis and motor control.
